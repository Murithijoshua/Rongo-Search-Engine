						Welcome Message					


						Rongo University established a Directorate of Open Distance and e-Learning (ODeL) to accord educational opportunities to learners who for some reason are not able to attend residential courses on campus. The unit has borrowed from best practices worldwide to roll out programs that focus on quality in material development and delivery, enhanced learner satisfaction and efficient and effective service delivery. Open Distance and e-Learning students receive the same quality of education as those on campus. In addition, the Directorate has structures to maximize interactions between learners and lecturers and also ensure that distance learning students interact among themselves, participants in the vibrant University life and access all University services including library, academic advising and career information. The Directorate admitted its new batch of students on the 15th January 2015. ODeL runs Programs both Post Graduate and Undergraduate from the school of Arts & Social Sciences, School of Education and School of Business and Human resource Development. Currently we offer the following courses:-
Diploma in Criminology and Security Studies
Diploma in social work and community development
Bachelor of Arts in Criminology and Security Studies
Bachelor of education Arts 
Bachelor of education early childhood and primary education (ECPE)
Bachelor of commerce
Bachelor of Science in Project Planning and Management
Master of Arts in Sociology
Holding on the ‘MOTO’’ ODeL-Flexible Education at Your Convenience. Rongo University ODeL mode is growing in students’ number and programmes. Currently the Directorate has a Centre in Sori nyatike Sub County Migori County with 59 students and ODeL Wajir Town in Wajir County. 
					


























open distance and e-learning board 



Prof. Wilson Otengah – Chairman 










				Prof. Otengah is the current Director of ODeL Directorate. He has PhD in Developmental Administration, a Master of Arts Degree in Sociology, a Post Graduate Diploma in Social Work, a Post Graduate diploma in Education and a Bachelors degree in Sociology. He is a professor of Sociology and Development Administration, with extensive teaching experience of 22 years at the University level. He previously taught at Kenyatta University from 1997 to 2013, where he was assistant director of Open Distance and e learning for four years.

While at Kenyatta University, he served as Departmental Chairman of the Board of Post Graduate Studies, member of the School of Humanities Board of Post Graduate Studies, and managed the Sociology Department’s resource center. He has extensive experience in the planning and facilitation of corporate training. He has widely published with vast experience as a researcher and scholar.

Prof Otengah has a rich experience on ODeL mode of learning which is a major resource base for the Directorate. 					












Dr. John Oluoch – Member 




				Dr. John Oluoch is currently the Director Privately Sponsored Students Programmes (PSSP) and Ag. Dean, School of Information, Communication and Media Studies at Rongo University. Dr. Oluoch has been a member of the Management Board of Rongo University since June 2012. He brings with him vast administration skills, having served as an administrative officer in different departments at Moi University.					












Dr. Henry D S Kinya – Member 




				Dr. Kinya is a senior lecturer in the department of information science school of information communication and media studies. He has worked as Senior Librarian at Africa Nazarene University- Nairobi, Kenya, Manager of Documentation and Office Services at Kenya Shell limited for a period of 9 years. Dr. Kinya is a trained library and information scientist, and has published books, research papers and presented papers at international conferences. He is a member of the Library and Information Association of South Africa (LIASA), America Library Association, Leadership in information science Forum (LIS) and Kenya National Library Association (KLA). He holds a PhD degree in library and information science.					












Phoebe Owiti (CText ATI)- Member 




				Edit Column
Phoebe, a Lecturer of Fashion Design and Textile Technology in the School of Science, Technology and Engineering. Her PhD research focuses on developing new methods of learning and teaching using 3D scanning, 3D graphics software and 3D CAD/CAM applications in Fashion and Textile Technology. She graduated from University of Huddersfield, UK with an MA in Fashion Design and Textiles, and is a holder of a Post Graduate Diploma in Education (Curriculum Design, Development, Instruction in Multi- Media Technology, Fashion Design and Textile Technology); a Higher National Diploma in Fashion Design and Textile Technology; and a BSc. in Fashion Design and Textiles. She is a Chartered Associate of The Textile Institute International (CText ATI) U.K and a Fellow of Fashion Group International, US. She has fifteen years of teaching experience in Universities in Kenya and Internationally. She has worked as a Lecturer of Apparel and Fashion Design at the University of Eldoret, Kenya; an Assistant Lecturer of Fashion Design and Textiles at Manchester Metropolitan University, UK; a Research Assistant in New Technologies in Fashion and Textiles at Manchester College of Arts and Technology (MANCAT).
